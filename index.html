Projects

Graymatics, Inc.: Designed an optimized LLM stack for custom data training on a local GPU cluster targeting enterprise customers.

At Graymatics, I focused on optimizing an LLM stack for custom data training on a local GPU cluster. This involved setting up the environment on Ubuntu, using Nvidia CUDA on a GeForce GPU, and implementing frameworks like PyTorch, Hugging Face, and Ollama.

Applause Meter: Engineered and synthesized intricate circuit schematics for implementation on breadboard prototypes, leading to a fully functioning prototype of an Applause Meter device.

The Applause Meter project involved creating an acoustic-responsive circuit mechanism that differentiated sound intensity to trigger reactions, particularly calibrated for hand clapping. The project was a great success, improving circuit efficiency by 15% and component usage by 20%.

AI Inferencing Engine Research: Led a pioneering research initiative to transition AI inferencing to edge computing, enhancing accessibility and reducing dependency on cloud infrastructure. Read My Blog!

My research on AI frameworks focused on transitioning to edge computing, using platforms like Intel CPU, Nvidia GPU, and Kinara ARM. The goal was to optimize latency for computer vision tasks, demonstrating superior performance on the Kinara ARM platform.
